PHASE ONE

IRON MAN

"Iron Man" was released in theaters on May 2, 2008.
The movie tells the story of Tony Stark, a billionaire industrialist and genius inventor, who is kidnapped and forced to build a weapon. Instead, he creates a technologically advanced suit of armor and escapes captivity. Returning home, he refines the suit and uses it to fight crime and protect the world as the superhero Iron Man.

THE INCREDIBLE HULK

"The Incredible Hulk" was released on June 13, 2008.
The film, starring Edward Norton as Bruce Banner and Liv Tyler as Betty Ross, is about Banner's quest to find a cure for the gamma radiation that turns him into the Hulk. He is on the run from General Thunderbolt Ross and the military, who want to capture him, and also faces a powerful new enemy called the Abomination.

IRON MAN 2

"Iron Man 2" was released in the United States on May 7, 2010.
The film is a sequel to Iron Man, and the third installment in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. In the movie, Tony Stark faces challenges including his own failing health, a new villain, Ivan Vanko, who seeks revenge, and the threat of rival defense contractor Justin Hammer.

THOR

"Thor", was released on May 6, 2011, in the United States.
The movie introduces the character Thor, a powerful but arrogant prince of Asgard, who is banished to Earth by his father, Odin, for his reckless behavior. While on Earth, he learns humility and begins his journey to become worthy of his magical hammer, Mjolnir, while his brother Loki plots to seize the throne of Asgard.

CAPTAIN AMERICAR: THE FIRST AVENGER

"Captain America: The First Avenger" was released on July 22, 2011, in the United States.
The movie is set during World War II and tells the story of Steve Rogers, a frail young man who volunteers for a secret experiment and becomes Captain America, a super-soldier. He then leads the fight against the Nazi-backed HYDRA organization.

ANT MAN & THE WASP

"Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania" was released on February 17, 2023.
The movie sees Ant-Man and the Wasp exploring the Quantum Realm with their family, encountering strange creatures and embarking on an adventure that pushes their limits. It is the first film of Phase Five in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

WANDAVISION

WandaVision premiered on Disney+ on January 15, 2021, and concluded on March 5, 2021.
The series is a Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) miniseries following Wanda Maximoff and Vision as they navigate a seemingly idyllic suburban life in the town of Westview. The series explores the mystery of their unusual situation, as they realize their reality is not what it seems, and it serves as a tie-in to the movie Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness.

BLACK-WIDOW

The Black Widow movie, a Marvel Cinematic Universe film, was released on July 9, 2021.
in theaters and on Disney+ with Premier Access. It's a prequel set between the events of Captain America: Civil War and Avengers: Infinity War, following Natasha Romanoff as she confronts her past and a dangerous conspiracy.

DOCTOR STRANGE IN THE MULTIVERSE OF MADNESS

"Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness" was released in theaters on May 6, 2022.
The movie is a sequel to the 2016 film "Doctor Strange" and the 28th film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). It sees Doctor Strange venturing into the multiverse, facing both old and new adversaries, and grappling with the consequences of his actions that have opened up dangerous realities.

AVENGERS(team)

THE AVENGERS

"The Avengers" was released on May 4, 2012, in the United States, and internationally on April 25, 2012.
The film is about a team of superheroes assembled to protect Earth from Loki and his alien army. It brings together Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Black Widow, and Hawkeye to face a common threat.

AVENGERS AGE OF ULTRON

"Avengers: Age of Ultron" was released in the United States on May 1, 2015.
In "Age of Ultron," Tony Stark's peacekeeping program, designed to protect the world, goes awry, creating the villainous Ultron. Ultron, believing humanity is the problem, seeks to destroy it, forcing the Avengers to reunite and stop him. The movie also introduces new characters, Wanda and Pietro Maximoff (Scarlet Witch and Quicksilver), who have their own complex relationship with Ultron and the Avengers. The film culminates in a battle to save the world from Ultron's destructive plan, with the Avengers facing difficult choices and personal sacrifices.

CAPTIAN AMERICA: CIVIL WAR

"Captain America: Civil War" was released on May 6, 2016, in the United States.
The film, which is the thirteenth installment in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), pits Captain America against Iron Man due to a disagreement over government oversight of the Avengers. This leads to a major conflict within the team, forcing them to choose sides.

AVENGERS: INFINITY WAR

Avengers: Infinity War was released on April 27, 2018.
The movie sees the Avengers and their allies facing off against the powerful Thanos, who is on a quest to collect the Infinity Stones and wield them to reshape reality. The film culminates in a devastating defeat for the heroes, as Thanos successfully wipes out half of all life in the universe.

AVENGERS: END GAME

Avengers: Endgame was released in theaters on April 26, 2019.
The film is a direct sequel to Avengers: Infinity War and the culmination of the Infinity Saga within the Marvel Cinematic Universe. It sees the remaining Avengers attempt to undo the devastation caused by Thanos after he wiped out half of all life.
